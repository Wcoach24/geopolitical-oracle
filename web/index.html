Analytical Framework
Ray Dalio's Debt Cycle Model
We apply Dalio's macroeconomic principle: debt cycles create predictable shifts in capital flows, interest rates, and asset valuations. Geopolitical events that disrupt energy supply, trade routes, or capital markets create leveraged debt positioning that forces rapid repricing. Example: EU sanctions on Russian energy created a debt servicing crisis in energy-dependent economies, creating alpha in commodity and currency pairs.
Robert Pape's Interstate Conflict Framework
We track Pape's work on the drivers of interstate conflict: territorial disputes, resource competition, and systemic power transitions. These factors predict geopolitical flashpoints 3-12 months before markets price them. The model weights: (1) capability differential between actors, (2) resource scarcity intensity, (3) credibility of commitment. High scores predict market dislocations in affected regions.
Supply Chain Vulnerability Index
We map critical supply chains (semiconductors, rare earths, pharmaceuticals) and overlay them with geopolitical risk zones. Disruption probabilities are weighted by: (1) concentration of production, (2) geographic fragility, (3) substitution elasticity. This creates real-time alerts for supply-side inflation and equity sector rotation opportunities.
Currency Carry Trade Mechanics
Geopolitical shocks unwind carry trades. We track positions in JPY, CHF, and other safe-haven currency pairs, and trigger shorts when geopolitical risk premia compress. Conversely, commodity-dependent currencies (AUD, CAD, RUB) become oversold during commodity booms triggered by disruption narratives.

Verification Process
All calls are timestamped publicly. Returns are calculated from market open the day after the call is published. We use official market data (CME, ICE, CBOE) to verify outcomes. No cherry-picking, no post-hoc adjustments. Losses are counted equally with wins.
